Difference-in-itself

DeleuzeOntology

Difference as primary and productive, not derived from identity.

Deleuze reframes ontology so that difference is ontologically primary: identity is an effect of differential processes. Counter to classical A=A primacy. Links: see Repetition; Becoming; Derrida: différance (contrast).

See also: repetition · becoming · differance

Repetition

DeleuzeTime

Repetition never returns the same; it is variation that produces singularities.

Against representational sameness, repetition is a creative divergence—each pass-through selects and differentiates. Identity across time = pattern of differing repetitions.

See also: difference-in-itself · becoming
↑

Becoming

DeleuzeProcess

No fixed being; continuous transformation (becoming-woman/animal-imperceptible).

Becoming is transversal and non-teleological. Subjects, species, and forms are thresholds in a flow, not essences. Links: Multiplicity; Assemblage; Lacan: lack (contrast).

See also: multiplicity · assemblage · lack
↑

Multiplicity

DeleuzeOntology

Open sets of relations rather than unified essences.

A multiplicity is an n-dimensional manifold of variable relations. The self is a multiplicity, not a unitary subject. Links: Assemblage; Rhizome.

See also: assemblage · rhizome
↑

Assemblage (Agencement)

DeleuzeGuattariSubjectivity

Provisional arrangement of heterogeneous elements that produces effects.

An assemblage is a functional coupling of bodies, discourses, tools, affects. Subjectivity is assembled and can be reassembled (territorialized/deterritorialized). Links: Deterritorialization; Platforms (application).

See also: deterritorialization · platforms
↑

Rhizome

DeleuzeGuattariTopology

Non-hierarchical, acentered network of connections.

Opposed to arborescent (tree) models. Rhizomes spread via plateaus and lines of flight. Identity arises as a node-effect in a network of becomings.

See also: assemblage · becoming
↑

Plane of Immanence

DeleuzeOntology

No transcendent grounding; concepts arise within life’s plane.

Philosophy creates concepts on a plane of immanence; no outside essence that fixes identity. Contrast with Lacan’s structuring of subject by the Symbolic.

See also: archi-writing · symbolic-order
↑

Societies of Control

DeleuzeMedia

From enclosures to continuous modulation (codes, access, metrics).

Identity becomes a dividual: sliced, modulated, scored over time across databases and platforms. Links: Protocols; Modulation; Iterability (correspondence).

See also: protocols · modulation · iterability

Heterogenesis

GuattariDeleuzeSubjectivity

Production through difference—continuous resingularization of subjectivity.

Named explicitly by Guattari; implicit in Deleuze. Subjectivity as collective, processual, opening to alterity. Links: Multiplicity; Assemblage; Lacan: objet petit a (tension).

See also: multiplicity · assemblage · objet-petit-a
↑

Deterritorialization

DeleuzeGuattariDynamics

Unbinding of structures that enables new assemblages (reterritorializations).

Identity shifts when territories dissolve and recompose. Lines of flight open onto novel couplings; always paired with reterritorialization.

See also: assemblage · reterritorialization
↑

différance

DerridaLanguage

Meaning arises via difference and deferral; never fully present.

The sign’s meaning is produced by differences from other signs and is deferred across chains of signification. The subject can’t coincide with itself as pure presence.

See also: trace · iterability · difference-in-itself
↑

Trace

DerridaLanguage

Remainder of other signs within each sign; no pure origin.

Every identity bears the mark of what it is not. Traces undo the possibility of a closed, self-identical meaning or subject.

See also: supplement · archi-writing
↑

Supplement

DerridaLogic

What adds also founds and destabilizes the thing.

The supplement shows that the ‘outside’ is constitutive, not secondary: identity relies on supplements that alter it.

See also: trace · deconstruction
↑

Iterability

DerridaLanguageMedia

Signs must be repeatable, but repetition always differs by context.

Identity depends on iterable marks (names, signatures, profiles) that can never guarantee identical return. Bridge to protocols and access control.

See also: protocols · control
↑

Deconstruction

DerridaMethod

Reading that reveals internal tensions in concepts and texts.

Not destruction; a careful tracing of conflicts that unsettle closure. Applied to identity, exposes constitutive contradictions.

See also: differance · supplement
↑

Archi-writing

DerridaLanguage

General condition of signification beyond speech/writing opposition.

Identity is ‘written’ by the systemic play of traces; undermines presence and inner essence. Links: Plane of immanence (analogy).

See also: trace · immanence
↑

Symbolic Order

LacanStructure

The network of signifiers structuring subjectivity and law.

Subjectivity is positioned by the Symbolic (S1, S2). Identity is an effect of signifier chains, not an inner essence. Bridge to Derrida’s textuality.

See also: signifier · name-of-the-father · differance
↑

Imaginary

LacanPsyche

Domain of images, misrecognition, ego formation (mirror stage).

Identity as image-based coherence; always a misrecognition. Contrasts with Deleuze’s anti-identitarian multiplicity.

See also: mirror-stage · multiplicity
↑

The Real

LacanLimit

That which resists symbolization; trauma and impossibility.

Identity meets a limit in the Real—points where signification fails. Contrast with Deleuzian becoming (which affirms flux).

See also: lack · objet-petit-a
↑

objet petit a

LacanDesire

Cause of desire; leftover remainder that propels subjectivity.

A heterogenetic lure that never coincides with the subject—links to Guattari’s heterogenesis as continuous resingularization.

See also: heterogenesis · lack
↑

Lack

LacanDesire

Structural absence produced by language; fuels desire.

For Lacan, subjectivity is structured by lack; for Deleuze/Guattari, desire is productive rather than lack-based (contrast).

See also: desiring-production · differance
↑

Signifier (S1/S2)

LacanLanguage

Master signifier (S1) anchors a chain of signifiers (S2).

Identity positions depend on signifying chains; anticipates Derrida’s critique of presence and aligns with textual construction of the subject.

See also: symbolic-order · trace
↑

Name-of-the-Father

LacanLaw

Signifier of law/prohibition structuring subjectivity.

Introduces subject into the Symbolic, instituting difference and prohibition. Cross-link with Derrida’s law and deconstruction of origins.

See also: symbolic-order · deconstruction
↑

Protocols

MediaBridge

Rules that enable/limit communication; authorization, addresses.

From Derrida’s iterability (repeatable marks) to Deleuze’s control (access), protocols shape digital identities. Passwords, tokens, signatures.

See also: iterability · control
↑

Modulation

MediaBridge

Continuous variation of parameters over time (scores, metrics).

Deleuzian control society notion; identity becomes divisible and continuously updated (dividual).

See also: control · iterability
↑

Platforms / Networks

MediaBridge

Distributed graphs where identity is a node-effect.

Rhizomatic connectivity combined with protocol governance; subjectivity as assembled across interfaces and data trails.

See also: rhizome · assemblage · protocols
